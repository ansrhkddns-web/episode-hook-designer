# Comment Trigger Points

Use this reference to design episode moments that make readers comment naturally. Comments should come from story pressure, not artificial prompts.

## Comment Principle

A good comment trigger gives readers a specific reaction target:

- predict
- choose sides
- get angry
- celebrate
- worry
- ship characters
- argue about a decision
- demand catharsis
- theorize about a secret

One strong target is better than many weak targets.

## Trigger Types

### Theory Trigger

Use when a mystery, hidden identity, regression clue, prophecy, system rule, or suspicious behavior appears.

- Design: give one concrete clue and one missing piece.
- Reader comment: "Is X actually Y?"
- Avoid: clues so vague that readers cannot guess.

### Side-Taking Trigger

Use when two characters have understandable but conflicting positions.

- Design: both sides have a reason, but one choice has immediate emotional cost.
- Reader comment: "I understand A, but B is right."
- Avoid: making one side cartoonishly wrong unless the goal is catharsis.

### Punishment Demand Trigger

Use when readers hate a target.

- Design: show the target crossing a line, then show the protagonist gaining a means to respond.
- Reader comment: "Please destroy them next episode."
- Avoid: repeated abuse without progress toward punishment.

### Shipping Trigger

Use when romance or emotional chemistry matters.

- Design: one action can be read as care, jealousy, protection, or confession-adjacent.
- Reader comment: "He definitely likes her."
- Avoid: ambiguity with no emotional movement.

### Decision Debate Trigger

Use when the protagonist faces a meaningful choice.

- Design: both choices have different rewards and costs.
- Reader comment: "Take the deal" / "Don't trust them."
- Avoid: fake choices.

### Public Reaction Trigger

Use when readers enjoy status change.

- Design: crowd, media, guild, family, class, market, or ranking reacts unexpectedly.
- Reader comment: "They finally noticed."
- Avoid: praise that adds no new stake.

### Betrayal Suspicion Trigger

Use when trust is unstable.

- Design: one ally does something explainable but suspicious.
- Reader comment: "Don't trust him."
- Avoid: betrayal signs with no later payoff.

### Meme-Line Trigger

Use sparingly for distinctive lines or behavior readers can quote.

- Design: give a character a sharp, repeatable reaction or declaration.
- Reader comment: quoting the line, laughing, repeating a catchphrase.
- Avoid: forcing jokes in scenes that need tension.

## Placement Rules

- Place a comment trigger near the ending when possible.
- Do not place it after tension has cooled.
- Pair comment triggers with visible stakes.
- Use comments to amplify the next-click question.

## Output Template

For each trigger, provide:

- trigger type
- story moment
- expected reader reaction
- sample comment direction
- risk
- how to make it less artificial
