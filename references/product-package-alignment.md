# Product Package Alignment

Use this reference when the user wants the webnovel to work as a product, not only as a story. The title, intro, tags, 1화, free episodes, and paid boundary should sell the same core promise.

## Product Promise

State the product promise in one sentence:

```text
This work sells [genre pleasure] through [protagonist advantage/desire] against [repeating pressure].
```

Korean output example:

```text
이 작품은 회귀 지식을 가진 주인공이 망한 가문과 원수를 상대로 선점과 응징을 반복하는 복수/성장 쾌감을 판다.
```

## Mismatch Types

| Mismatch | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Title promises power fantasy, episode delivers passive suffering | readers feel baited | move protagonist agency and first proof earlier |
| Intro promises revenge, early episodes deliver only family drama | catharsis delayed too long | add leverage, evidence, or small punishment |
| Tags promise romance, scenes deliver no relationship movement | shipping energy absent | add public choice, jealousy, care, or boundary crossing |
| 1화 promises system/growth, next episodes explain politics | engine disappears | use system/growth to solve political pressure |
| Free final promises payoff, paid first delays it | payment trust breaks | pay the promised scene early, then open next layer |

## Package Audit Questions

- Would the target reader understand the genre from title/intro/1화?
- Does 1화 prove the title promise in scene form?
- Do episodes 2-5 prove the repeatable reward?
- Does the free section give enough trust before asking for payment?
- Does the paid section continue the same pleasure at a larger scale?

## Fix Priority

1. Align title/intro promise with 1화's first visible reward.
2. Remove or delay early material that sells a different genre.
3. Make the first five episodes repeat the promised pleasure.
4. Make the paid boundary pay a promised scene, not only open one.

Do not rewrite packaging in isolation. Packaging and episode hooks must reinforce each other.
