# Hook Pattern Library

Use this reference to choose a cliffhanger pattern or repair a weak episode ending. Prefer concrete hooks that change the situation over vague ominous mood.

## Selection Rule

Pick the hook that best answers:

- What did the reader almost get?
- What new cost, danger, identity, or choice interrupts it?
- What exact question will make the reader click?

Do not use the same hook type repeatedly unless the consequence changes.

## Core Patterns

### Reward-Blocked Hook

Use when the protagonist is about to receive a desired reward.

- Shape: reward appears -> obstacle reveals price -> episode ends before decision or consequence.
- Works for: rank-up, skill awakening, confession, money, revenge, rescue, exam result, contract.
- Next-click question: "Will the protagonist still take the reward after seeing the cost?"
- Avoid: blocking every reward so often that readers feel cheated.

### Forced-Choice Hook

Use when the protagonist must choose under pressure.

- Shape: two bad/good options appear -> both have visible cost -> protagonist is forced to answer now.
- Works for: survival, romance, politics, academy, revenge, business, hunter raids.
- Next-click question: "Which option will they choose, and what will they sacrifice?"
- Avoid: choices where one answer is obviously correct.

### Identity-Reveal Hook

Use when a hidden identity or relationship changes the meaning of prior events.

- Shape: clue or witness appears -> name/face/title is exposed -> episode ends at recognition.
- Works for: regression, possession, villainess, secret heir, hidden boss, stalker, traitor.
- Next-click question: "What happens now that this person knows or is known?"
- Avoid: revealing a name the reader has no reason to care about.

### Consequence-Arrives Hook

Use after the protagonist takes bold action.

- Shape: protagonist wins or crosses a line -> immediate consequence arrives -> response delayed.
- Works for: revenge, office politics, academy ranking, martial arts challenge, public humiliation.
- Next-click question: "Can they survive the backlash?"
- Avoid: consequences that feel disconnected from the action.

### Witness Hook

Use when public reaction matters.

- Shape: protagonist acts privately or unexpectedly -> someone important sees it -> social meaning changes.
- Works for: romance, academy, entertainment, idol, legal/medical/professional genres.
- Next-click question: "How will the witness use what they saw?"
- Avoid: random witnesses with no power.

### Timer Hook

Use when urgency is needed.

- Shape: goal appears -> deadline appears -> first move is required immediately.
- Works for: tower, gate, survival, exams, trials, missions, competitions.
- Next-click question: "Can they solve it before time runs out?"
- Avoid: timers without visible penalty.

### System-Message Hook

Use for game-system, hunter, tower, status-window, or regression stories.

- Shape: result appears -> system message contradicts expectation -> new rule or penalty appears.
- Works for: skills, quests, class changes, hidden achievements, rankings.
- Next-click question: "What does this new rule mean in action?"
- Avoid: long stat dumps without narrative consequence.

### Emotional-Line Hook

Use when a relationship changes.

- Shape: emotional tension builds -> confession, accusation, betrayal, or boundary crossing happens -> answer is delayed.
- Works for: romance, rofan, BL/GL, found family, rivalry, betrayal.
- Next-click question: "How will the other person respond?"
- Avoid: ending on internal monologue after the line lands.

### Villain-Move Hook

Use when the antagonist should feel active.

- Shape: protagonist believes they gained ground -> villain reveals a countermeasure -> next conflict begins.
- Works for: revenge, politics, fantasy war, business, legal, academy factions.
- Next-click question: "How will the protagonist overturn the counter?"
- Avoid: villains who only threaten without action.

### Evidence-Twist Hook

Use when information changes the reader's interpretation.

- Shape: investigation or assumption progresses -> new evidence proves the premise wrong -> episode ends at reinterpretation.
- Works for: mystery, thriller, legal, medical, horror, political intrigue.
- Next-click question: "What is the real truth behind this?"
- Avoid: evidence too abstract to visualize.

### Public-Reaction Hook

Use when readers enjoy status change.

- Shape: protagonist acts -> crowd/market/media/rankers react -> unexpected reaction escalates the stakes.
- Works for: entertainment, idol, sports, academy, hunter ranking, streaming, business.
- Next-click question: "How far will the reaction spread?"
- Avoid: praise with no next problem.

### Punishment-Promise Hook

Use when readers want catharsis.

- Shape: hated target commits or is exposed -> protagonist gains means to punish -> execution is delayed.
- Works for: revenge, villainess, office, academy bullying, family conflict.
- Next-click question: "How satisfying will the punishment be?"
- Avoid: delaying punishment too long without smaller catharsis.

## Repair Patterns

If the ending is quiet, move the strongest event from the next episode to the last 10-20% of the current episode.

If the ending is only mystery, add consequence or reward proximity.

If the ending is only danger, add a personal stake or irreversible cost.

If the ending is only reward, reveal the price.

If the ending is only a conversation, end on the line that forces an answer.
