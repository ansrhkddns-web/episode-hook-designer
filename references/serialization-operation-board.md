# Serialization Operation Board

Use this reference when the user wants a production-oriented board for drafting, backlog planning, or release readiness. This is not project management for its own sake. It exists to protect hooks, payoff timing, and paid trust during ongoing serialization.

## Board Principle

Each planned episode should have a status for:

- draft readiness
- reward payment
- ending hook
- next episode handoff
- risk level

If these are unknown, the episode is not ready for efficient drafting.

## Status Definitions

- `idea`: concept exists, not draftable.
- `brief`: episode has function, reward, hook, and handoff.
- `draft`: prose exists but not validated.
- `revise`: needs structural or hook repair.
- `ready`: passes reward, hook, handoff, and package alignment checks.

## Backlog Rule

For commercial writing, plan at least the next 3 hooks:

- current episode ending hook
- next episode payoff
- next episode ending hook

This prevents writing a strong cliffhanger with no satisfying follow-up.

## Release Readiness Check

Before marking an episode ready:

- previous hook is answered or advanced
- current episode gives reward
- current ending creates specific next-click question
- next episode can pay or advance that question
- reward debt is tracked
- no paid promise is being dodged

## Production Risk Labels

- `low`: polish-level issue.
- `medium`: hook or reward needs targeted fix.
- `high`: episode cannot be drafted cleanly.
- `critical`: paid trust, genre promise, or protagonist agency is broken.

If risk is high or critical, do not recommend proceeding to prose until the brief is repaired.
