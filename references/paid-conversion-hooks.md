# Paid Conversion Hooks

Use this reference when designing the free-to-paid boundary. The goal is to make payment feel like the natural next step, not a trick.

## Conversion Principle

The free final episode should create a strong unpaid promise. The first paid episode should pay that promise quickly, then open a larger desire.

Do not make readers pay only to receive information that should have been included in the free episode. Give them an event, confrontation, reward, reveal, punishment, confession, or irreversible turn.

## Free Final Episode Rules

The free final episode should:

- deliver a satisfying partial reward before the end
- expose a bigger reward, danger, or emotional answer
- stop at the point where the next action matters
- make the reader say what they want to see next

Strong endings:

- the protagonist gets the key but sees the locked door
- the villain is exposed but not punished yet
- the love interest asks the question but the answer is delayed
- the ranking changes and summons a stronger opponent
- the system grants a reward with an alarming condition
- the target finally appears in the same room

Weak endings:

- the protagonist decides to prepare
- a vague threat approaches
- a secret is mentioned but not contextualized
- the episode ends after the emotional peak is already resolved
- the first paid episode would only explain what the hook means

## First Paid Episode Rules

The first paid episode should:

- pay off the exact promise from the free final hook early
- give a visible result that justifies payment
- avoid spending the whole episode on recap or setup
- open a larger problem after payoff

Recommended structure:

1. Resolve or advance the free-final question within the first 30-40%.
2. Show concrete reward, damage, reaction, confession, or reveal.
3. Let the result create a larger problem.
4. End on a new hook that proves the paid section has momentum.

## Conversion Models

### Catharsis-Delayed

Use when the reader wants punishment or victory.

- Free final: hated target is cornered.
- Paid first: punishment begins or the first blow lands.
- Larger hook: the target's backer appears or the punishment uncovers a bigger enemy.

### Reward-Price

Use when the reader wants growth, rank, power, money, or romance confirmation.

- Free final: reward appears.
- Paid first: protagonist obtains or refuses it with a visible consequence.
- Larger hook: reward has a hidden price, condition, or watcher.

### Truth-Door

Use for mystery, regression, possession, conspiracy, or hidden identity.

- Free final: first answer is revealed.
- Paid first: the answer changes the protagonist's next action.
- Larger hook: the answer points to a bigger locked truth.

### Relationship-Answer

Use for romance, rofan, BL/GL, rivalry, family, or betrayal.

- Free final: one character asks, confesses, accuses, or witnesses.
- Paid first: the relationship changes visibly.
- Larger hook: the new relationship creates external pressure.

### Arena-Entrance

Use for academy, tower, hunter, martial arts, competition, professional, or business genres.

- Free final: protagonist qualifies, is summoned, or enters the arena.
- Paid first: first clash proves the new arena's fun.
- Larger hook: the hierarchy notices.

## Fairness Check

Before finalizing, answer:

- What exactly is the reader paying to see?
- Does the first paid episode actually show it?
- Is there a visible reward within the paid episode?
- Does the new problem feel earned by the payoff?
- Would the reader feel teased or rewarded?

If the answer is mostly "teased", add a concrete payoff to the first paid episode.
