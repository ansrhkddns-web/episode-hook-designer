# Reader Reward Ledger

Use this reference to manage reader trust across episodes. Commercial webnovels survive on repeated proof that waiting pays.

## Reward Debt

Reward debt is created when an episode promises a payoff but does not deliver it yet.

Healthy debt:

- created after a visible reward
- tied to a specific next question
- has a near-term payoff path
- increases anticipation

Unhealthy debt:

- stacks many mysteries without answers
- delays punishment after repeated frustration
- moves promised scenes away after payment
- opens new questions before giving proof

## Reward Types

Use varied rewards:

- growth: level, skill, technique, competence
- status: rank, reputation, title, public recognition
- resource: money, item, land, contract, access
- relationship: trust, jealousy, alliance, confession pressure
- knowledge: clue, rule, secret, identity, future change
- catharsis: punishment, rescue, humiliation reversal, victory
- safety: escape, protection, sanctuary, survival
- agency: choice, refusal, negotiation, counterattack

Avoid repeating the same reward form for too many episodes.

## Payoff Timing

General guidance:

- 1화: create click question and first proof.
- 2-5화: pay at least one small promise.
- Every 2-4 episodes: give a visible small reward.
- Every 8-12 episodes: give a larger turn, catharsis, reveal, or relationship shift.
- Paid first episode: pay the free-final hook early.

This is guidance, not a fixed formula. If the genre expects rapid payoff, shorten the interval.

## Trust Risk Signals

Mark risk when:

- the same villain escapes without cost repeatedly
- the protagonist loses agency for multiple episodes
- the story teases a secret but gives no usable clue
- paid chapters begin by avoiding the promised scene
- rewards are only numbers, not changed scenes
- comments would likely say "when does this actually happen?"

## Ledger-Based Revision

When trust risk is high:

1. Identify the oldest unpaid promise.
2. Decide whether to pay, partially answer, or cancel it.
3. Give a concrete reward before opening another major question.
4. Attach the next hook to the payoff's consequence.

Do not solve reward debt by adding a bigger mystery.
