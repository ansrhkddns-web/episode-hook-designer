# Episode Diagnostics Rubric

Use this reference to diagnose an existing episode outline, draft, or ending. Score for click behavior, not literary elegance.

## Score Scale

Use 0-5:

- 0: absent
- 1: present but vague
- 2: functional but weak
- 3: acceptable
- 4: strong
- 5: very strong and commercially useful

## Rubric

### Genre Promise

Does the episode prove the genre's expected fun?

- 0-1: genre could be anything
- 2-3: genre is visible but not exciting
- 4-5: genre pleasure is concrete and repeatable

Fix weak scores by adding a scene where the premise works in action.

### Protagonist Agency

Does the protagonist choose, act, risk, deceive, fight, investigate, negotiate, confess, refuse, or sacrifice?

- 0-1: protagonist observes
- 2-3: protagonist acts but without cost
- 4-5: protagonist action changes the situation

Fix weak scores by making the protagonist cause the ending pressure.

### Episode Reward

Does the reader receive something concrete in this episode?

- 0-1: only setup
- 2-3: reward exists but is internal or abstract
- 4-5: reward is visible through result, reaction, status, answer, or catharsis

Fix weak scores by externalizing the reward.

### Unfinished Pressure

Does the ending leave a sharp reason to continue?

- 0-1: no open pressure
- 2-3: open pressure exists but is vague
- 4-5: reader knows exactly what they want next

Fix weak scores by turning mood into a concrete event, reveal, choice, or consequence.

### Hook Specificity

Can the next-click question be written in one sentence?

- 0-1: "What happens next?" only
- 2-3: general curiosity
- 4-5: specific question with clear object and stakes

Fix weak scores by naming the object: who, what, what cost, what answer, what punishment, what reward.

### Comment Potential

Does the episode give readers something to react to?

- 0-1: nothing to discuss
- 2-3: mild reaction
- 4-5: prediction, anger, shipping, side-taking, celebration, or theory material

Fix weak scores by adding a decision, unfairness, clue, line, witness, or relationship signal.

### Paid Fairness

Use only around paid conversion.

- 0-1: pure bait
- 2-3: clickable but may feel unfair
- 4-5: free final creates desire and paid first gives payoff

Fix weak scores by adding an early payoff to the first paid episode.

## Common Problems and Fixes

### Problem: The episode ends after the result.

Fix: end at the arrival of consequence, witness, price, or next choice.

### Problem: The episode is all explanation.

Fix: make the explanation discovered during a conflict or test.

### Problem: The hook is vague.

Fix: replace mood with an object, message, name, rule, timer, person, accusation, or visible change.

### Problem: The protagonist is passive.

Fix: make the protagonist's action trigger the complication.

### Problem: The reader receives no reward.

Fix: add a small but visible win before the ending.

### Problem: The paid hook feels manipulative.

Fix: pay off the free-final question early in the first paid episode, then open the next layer.
